Men's Title Contenders

The 2026 WSL Championship Tour men's field is stacked with talent across multiple generations. John John Florence of Hawaii enters as the defending world champion and clear favorite. His ability to perform in both small-wave technical events and heavy water locations like Pipeline and Teahupo'o gives him a consistency edge that few can match. Florence has won three world titles and shows no signs of slowing down at 33.

Griffin Colapinto represents the next generation of American surfing and finished second in the 2025 standings. His progressive aerial game combined with improved rail work has made him a legitimate title threat at every stop. Colapinto's strength at California events like Lower Trestles, where the WSL Finals are held, gives him a strategic advantage in the end-of-season format.

Ethan Ewing from Australia has quietly built himself into a perennial top-five surfer. His backhand surfing at locations like Bells Beach and Margaret River is considered the best on tour. If the 2026 season features good swell at the Australian and Tahitian legs, Ewing could be the biggest threat to Florence's crown.

Italo Ferreira, the 2019 world champion from Brazil, remains dangerous in every event he enters. His explosive style and never-say-die competitive attitude make him capable of winning any heat on any day. Ferreira's challenge has always been consistency across an entire season, but his talent ceiling is as high as anyone's on tour.

Women's Title Contenders

Caroline Marks enters the 2026 season as the woman to beat after winning her second consecutive world title in 2025. The American surfer's combination of power, speed, and aerial ability has redefined what's possible in women's competitive surfing. At just 24, Marks is approaching her physical peak and shows no weaknesses in any wave type.

Caitlin Simmers has been called the most naturally talented surfer on the women's tour. Her smooth, flowing style belies an aggressive competitive instinct that has produced multiple event victories. Simmers' improvement in the Tahitian event at Teahupo'o in 2025 removed what had been her biggest weakness, making her the most complete challenger to Marks.

Brisa Hennessy from Costa Rica and Molly Picklum from Australia round out the top tier. Hennessy's powerful backhand surfing and mental toughness in critical heats have made her a consistent top-three finisher. Picklum's progression over the past two seasons has been remarkable, with her combination of tube riding and progressive maneuvers earning increasingly higher scores from judges.

Dark Horses and Breakout Surfers

Every CT season produces at least one surfer who exceeds expectations and crashes into the title conversation. In 2026, watch for Brazilian prodigy Samuel Pupo, who earned his CT qualification through a dominant Challenger Series campaign. Pupo's air game rivals anyone on tour, and his brother Miguel's experience on the CT provides an invaluable support system.

Liam O'Brien from Australia has been knocking on the door of the top five for two seasons. His tube riding at Pipeline and Teahupo'o is world class, and a strong result at either of those events could catapult him into title contention. O'Brien's physical conditioning has improved markedly, giving him the stamina to maintain form across the long CT season.

On the women's side, Gabriela Bryan of Hawaii represents a genuine dark horse. Her local knowledge at Pipeline and Sunset Beach gives her an edge at the Hawaiian events, and her air game has developed rapidly. Bryan's challenge is translating her freesurf ability into consistent competitive results under pressure.

Key Events That Will Decide the Title

The Pipeline Masters in January sets the tone for the entire season. Hawaii's North Shore delivers the most consequential waves on tour, and performing well in heavy barrels builds confidence that carries through the year. Champions who start strong at Pipeline have historically maintained their momentum through the mid-season cut.

The Tahiti Pro at Teahupo'o in August is the season's most dramatic event. The heavy, shallow reef break separates genuine watermen from aerialists, and the event's position late in the calendar means results here directly impact finals qualification. Every surfer on tour knows that a poor result at Teahupo'o can end their title hopes.

The WSL Finals at Lower Trestles represent the culmination of the entire season. The top five ranked surfers compete in a single-day, single-elimination format. Lower Trestles' high-performance wave favors progressive surfing, meaning surfers with strong aerial games and variety in their repertoire tend to excel in the finals format.

For those interested in how maritime weather patterns affect wave forecasting, understanding ocean swell models is crucial to predicting which events will favor power surfers versus technical surfers. The interaction between distant storms and local bathymetry determines wave quality at each CT venue.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the favorite to win the WSL 2026 Championship Tour?

John John Florence enters 2026 as the consensus favorite after his dominant 2025 season. His combination of aerial ability, tube riding skill, and competitive experience at the highest level makes him the surfer to beat across all CT stops.

How many events are on the 2026 WSL Championship Tour?

The 2026 WSL Championship Tour features 10 regular season events across six countries, followed by the WSL Finals at Lower Trestles. Events span from January through September, with the mid-season cut reducing the field after event five.

What is the WSL mid-season cut?

The mid-season cut, introduced in 2022, eliminates roughly one-third of CT surfers after the fifth event. Only the top-ranked surfers continue competing for the world title, while eliminated surfers drop to the Challenger Series to re-qualify.

Where are the 2026 WSL Finals held?

The WSL Finals are held at Lower Trestles in San Clemente, California. The top five men and top five women compete in a single-day elimination format to determine the world champions.

Can you bet on WSL surfing events?

Yes, prediction markets and sportsbooks increasingly offer WSL surfing markets. Platforms like Kalshi offer event-level and season-long prediction markets, while traditional sportsbooks offer outright winner and head-to-head matchup odds for major CT events.
