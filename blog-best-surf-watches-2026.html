Best Overall Surf Watches

Garmin Instinct 2 Surf ($350) — The most complete surf watch available. Built-in tide data for thousands of surf spots worldwide. Surf activity profile tracks wave count, max speed, and distance per wave. GPS maps your lineup position and tracks paddle-outs. 24-day battery life in smartwatch mode, 30 hours in GPS mode. MIL-STD-810 shock and water resistance to 100m. The only watch that gives you a complete picture of every session.

Apple Watch Ultra 2 ($799) — Premium smartwatch with outstanding surf capabilities through the Dawn Patrol app. 36-hour battery, 100m water resistance, and the brightest display of any smartwatch — readable in direct sunlight while paddling. Tracks wave count, longest wave, top speed, and session duration. Also works as a full smartwatch for notifications, music, and health tracking. Expensive but the best all-around wrist computer for surfers who want everything.

Rip Curl SearchGPS 2 ($300) — Purpose-built by surfers for surfers. Automatic wave detection tracks wave count, speed, distance, and calories without pressing any buttons. Tide charts for 1,400+ surf breaks. Syncs with the Rip Curl app for detailed session analysis. 25-hour GPS battery. The most intuitive surf-specific watch on the market.

Best Budget Options

Casio G-Shock G-LIDE GBX-100 ($150) — Tide and moon data for any location. Bluetooth connectivity syncs with your phone for tide chart updates. Shock-resistant, 200m water resistance, and nearly indestructible. No GPS wave tracking, but the tide function is reliable. Solar-powered option (GBX-100CS) never needs charging. The watch of choice for surfers who want toughness over tech.

Nixon Regulus ($125) — Clean digital design with dual chronograph, custom alarms, and 100m water resistance. No surf-specific features but built for action sports durability. Polycarbonate case is lightweight and nearly indestructible. Available in a huge range of colors. A solid everyday watch that handles surf conditions without worry.

Key Features Explained

  • Wave count: Accelerometer and GPS detect when you catch a wave. Accuracy varies — Garmin and Rip Curl are 85-90% accurate, Apple Watch with Dawn Patrol is about 80%
  • Tide charts: Real-time tide data for your local break. Essential for planning sessions around optimal tidal conditions. NOAA-sourced data is the most accurate
  • GPS tracking: Maps your paddle-outs, wave rides, and lineup position. Shows your speed on each wave and total distance paddled. Fascinating data for session review
  • Water resistance: 100m minimum for surfing. 200m provides extra margin for wipeouts in heavy surf. ISO 6425 dive rating is the gold standard
  • Battery life: GPS tracking drains batteries fast. Look for 15+ hours in GPS mode to cover a full day of surfing with margin

Surf Watch vs Phone Apps

Free apps like Surfline Sessions and Dawn Patrol offer wave tracking through your phone. However, bringing your phone into the lineup risks water damage, theft, and distraction. A dedicated surf watch stays on your wrist, starts tracking automatically, and lets you stay in the moment. For casual surfers, phone apps are fine. For dedicated surfers, a GPS watch transforms how you understand your progression.

Care and Maintenance

  • Rinse after every session: Salt water corrodes buttons and sensors over time. 30 seconds under fresh water extends your watch's life significantly
  • Check seals annually: If your watch has user-replaceable batteries, have the seals tested when changing batteries. A failed seal means a dead watch
  • Avoid hot water: Hot showers can compromise rubber gaskets. Rinse with cool or lukewarm water only
  • Update firmware: Manufacturers regularly improve wave detection accuracy and add surf spots to tide databases through software updates

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a surf watch worth it?

If you surf regularly (weekly or more), a GPS surf watch provides valuable data on wave count, speed, and session analysis that helps you track progression. The Garmin Instinct 2 Surf at $350 is the best value for dedicated surfers.

Can I surf with an Apple Watch?

Yes. Apple Watch Series 7 and later have sufficient water resistance for surfing. The Apple Watch Ultra is the best Apple option with 100m rating and the brightest display. Use the Dawn Patrol app for wave tracking.

What is the most durable surf watch?

The Casio G-Shock G-LIDE series is nearly indestructible with MIL-STD-810 shock resistance and 200m water resistance. For GPS watches, the Garmin Instinct 2 Surf is the most rugged option.

Do surf watches count waves accurately?

Modern GPS surf watches are 80-90% accurate in wave counting. They occasionally miss small waves or count large duck-dives as waves. Accuracy improves with each firmware update.

How deep can I wear a surf watch?

Most surf watches are rated to 100-200m. For normal surfing, even heavy wipeouts, a 100m rating is more than sufficient. You would need to dive deliberately to approach these depths.
